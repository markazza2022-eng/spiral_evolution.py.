Spiral Curvature Evolution

A Dynamic Interpretation of Gravitational Structure

By Mark J Azzopardi

Abstract

This paper presents an extension to gravitational interpretation within a relativistic framework, introducing the concept of Spiral Curvature Evolution. While standard models such as General Relativity describe gravity as curvature of spacetime induced by mass-energy, this work emphasizes that rotational dynamics and energy flow play a central role in shaping the evolution of that curvature.

1. Introduction

Modern gravitational theory describes spacetime as a geometric manifold. However, real astrophysical systems exhibit rotation and energy transfer. This motivates a refined interpretation: Gravitational curvature is dynamically shaped by rotational motion and energy redistribution over time.

2. Theoretical Foundation

The standard field equations are defined as:

$$G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}$$

We utilize a relativistic fluid description where rotation is intrinsic to the velocity field $u^\mu$:

$$T^{\mu\nu} = (\rho + p)u^\mu u^\nu + p g^{\mu\nu}$$

3. Spiral Curvature Principle

Rotating systems are characterized by vorticity:

$$\omega_{\mu\nu} = \nabla_{[\mu} u_{\nu]}$$

Spiral structures emerge from the interaction of radial gravity and tangential angular momentum.

4. Evolution Mechanism

  • Phase I: Diffuse Distribution.
  • Phase II: Rotational Structuring.
  • Phase III: Compression Regime.
  • Phase IV: Redistribution (Radiation/Jets).
  • Phase V: Re-expansion.

5. Mathematical Formulation

We propose a modified Field Equation to represent the rotational "twist" of spacetime:

$$G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu} + \lambda \Theta_{\mu\nu}$$

Where $\Theta_{\mu\nu} = \omega_{\mu\alpha}\omega^{\alpha}_{\ \nu}$.
